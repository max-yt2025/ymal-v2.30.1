101 Discord Safety

Understanding the basics of Discord safety is the first step to protecting yourself and your community. Here's what every Discord user should know:

  • Never share your token — Anyone with your token can control your account.
  • Enable 2FA — Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security.
  • Be careful with links — Scammers use fake Nitro and game giveaways to steal accounts.
  • Verify bots — Only invite bots from trusted sources with proper verification.
  • Use moderation bots — Automated tools like YMAL Anti-Raid can protect your server 24/7.

How to Keep Your Discord Server Safe

Running a Discord server comes with responsibilities. Here's how to protect your community from raids, spam, and malicious activity:

  • Set up verification levels — Use phone verification or email verification for new members.
  • Use moderation bots — Bots like YMAL Anti-Raid can automatically detect and stop threats.
  • Create a moderation team — Train trusted members to handle incidents quickly.
  • Use raid protection — Enable slowmode, verification gates, and lockdown tools.
  • Regularly audit roles — Remove unused roles and ensure admins have 2FA enabled.

How to Keep Your Discord Account Safe

Your Discord account holds access to all your servers and messages. Here's how to keep it secure:

  • Enable 2FA — Use Google Authenticator or Authy for two-factor authentication.
  • Use a strong password — Avoid common passwords and use a password manager.
  • Never click suspicious links — Fake Nitro and game giveaways are common scams.
  • Don't share your token — Your token is like a password — keep it secret.
  • Revoke unused sessions — Regularly check active sessions in Discord settings.
